About The Endless Beauty Contest

By: Vanessa Archambault, Assaf Bar-Natan, and Nick Doiron

In the 1996 redistricting court case Bush v. Vera, the United States Supreme Court ruled that districts "may pass strict scrutiny without having to defeat rival compact districts designed by plaintiffs' experts in endless 'beauty contests,'..."

Following this sentiment, we have decided to engage the public in endless beauty contests of existing districts. This website was made in the two-day hackathon in the Boston MGGG workshop on gerrymandering. The districts shown in the main page are the 115th US congressional districts (retrieved August, 8, 2017), and should by no means be used as accurate depictions of current district shapes. This website is made purely for fun, and to see what shapes look good compared to others. Keep in mind that no district is drawn in context, and that "bad boundaries" may be affected by coastlines.
